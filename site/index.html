Research Case Studies

This website contains a number of extended case studies on psychological research and statistics. The objective is to show how statistical analysis is embedded in the research cycle.

Word interpretation

How does personal experience shape our understanding of words? Evidence from several sources show that learning mechanisms make critical contributions to our ability to interpret ambiguous words.

Go to this study »

Information search

How do we search for information to reduce our uncertainty? Do we search for evidence from diverse sources? And how important is the reliability of the evidence? This case study is about an experiment in which participants choose which witness to interview in a crime investigation.

Go to this study »

Visual Plasticity

How does auditory deprivation affect the functional architecture of early visual areas? This case study is about an experiment in which deaf and hearing participants are compared in central and periphal visual processing.

About ReCaSt

The ReCaSt project was funded by a teaching innovation grant to Dr Maarten Speekenbrink from the School of Life and Medical Sciences (SLMS), University College London.

People

Maarten Speekenbrink: Project leader

Maarten Speekenbrink is a lecturer in mathematical psychology at the department of Cognitive, Perceptual and Brain Sciences, University College London. His main research interests are in the areas of learning and decision making. He teaches a postgraduate course on statistics.

Rebecca Lyness: Author

Rebecca Lyness is a PhD student at the department of Cognitive, Perceptual and Brain Sciences, University College London.

Peter Riefer: Author

Peter Riefer is a PhD student at the department of Cognitive, Perceptual and Brain Sciences, University College London. His research focuses on group behaviour and particularly cooperation.
