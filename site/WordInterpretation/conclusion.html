Conclusion

We argued that the interpretation of words in a particular context is related to the salience of that context in a person's life. This refers to the frequency, recency and overall exposure to a group or environment, in which words are interpreted differently compared to the primary meaning in the general public. In our experiment, we studied the interpretation of words by rowers and tested their interpretation of twenty-one ambigous words, which can have different meanings in the rowing context. Regarding this, we found that recency and frequency of contextual exposure had the highest impact on the interpretation of words. The more recently and the more frequently subjects had rowed, the more of the twenty-one words they interpreted in a rowing context. Overall experience with rowing didn't seem to matter for this.
