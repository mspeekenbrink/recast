Method

Participants

A total of 215 rowers were recruited from three Cambridge (UK) rowing clubs, of which 57% were female. The rowers’ ages ranged between 16 and 76 years, with a mean age of 31.9 years (SD=13.7 years). An incentive of £100 was offered if 30 members completed the survey, and £125 if 40 members completed the survey. In addition to the rowers, 27 control subjects were recruited, who received the same word interpretation task as the rowers. The control group was necessary to assess whether rowers actually have a peculiar (i.e., biased) interpretation of words compared to non-rowers. Of the non-rowers, 56% were female and their age ranged between 19 and 48 with a mean age of 30.1 years (SD=9.2 years). There was no significant difference between the control and rower groups in terms of age (t=-.680, df=240, p>.05) and gender (Χ2=.014, df=1, p>.05) distribution.

Procedure

The online survey platform SurveyMonkey was used in order to present the questions to participants. Participants were told that the aim of the research was to discover the impact of exercise on memory. This allowed provided a plausible cover story for the specific recruitment of rowers without making them aware that they were being target specifically because of their rowing experience. The questionnaire consisted of a number of sections:

(I) Demographic information
The participants were asked to provide basic demographic information (age and gender) and their language background. To maintain the cover story they were also asked how often they exercised.

(II) Word Association Task
The participants completed a word-association task which consisted of one-hundred words; twenty-one of these were homographs with a secondary rowing meaning, the remaining 79 words were unrelated fillers, included to distract from the true aim of the experiment. For each word, participants were instructed to type in the first word that was related in meaning to the target word that came to their mind.

(III) Memory Test
The participants were given a memory test which consisted of twenty-four words; twelve of these words had been in the previous word-association task. This was included to maintain the cover story that this was primarily a short-term memory experiment.

(IV) Rowing habits
Participants were asked (i) how long they had participated in rowing (years); (ii) how many days since they had last participated in rowing; (iii) on average how many times per week they had participated in rowing over the last month.

(V) Aim of the study
Participants were asked the aim of the study to determine the extent the cover story had successfully diverted participants from the true aim.
